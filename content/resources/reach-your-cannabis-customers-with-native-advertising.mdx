title: 'Reach your cannabis customers with Native advertising'
date: 'Jan 1, 2023'

---

Native advertising refers to the practice of delivering targeted and personalized advertising to consumers in a way that is consistent with the form and function of the platform on which the advertising appears. This can include sponsored content, in-feed ads, and recommended content, among other forms. Native advertising offers several benefits for advertisers.

One of the main benefits of native advertising is its ability to increase engagement with consumers. By blending in with the surrounding content and not appearing intrusive or disruptive, native ads are able to capture the attention and interest of consumers in a way that traditional banner ads may not. This can lead to higher levels of engagement and a more positive response from consumers.

Another benefit of native advertising is its potential to increase trust with consumers. Because native ads are delivered in a way that is consistent with the editorial content of the platform, they can be perceived as more trustworthy and credible by consumers. This can lead to higher levels of brand trust and a stronger emotional connection with consumers.

Native advertising also allows advertisers to target their ads to specific audiences based on demographics, interests, and browsing habits. This enables advertisers to create highly relevant and personalized ads that are more likely to resonate with their target audience.

Finally, native advertising often has higher click-through rates and lower bounce rates compared to traditional banner ads. This can lead to improved performance for advertisers looking to drive traffic and conversions.

In summary, native advertising is an effective way for advertisers to deliver targeted and personalized advertising to consumers in a non-intrusive way. It can increase engagement, trust, and performance, making it a valuable tool for advertisers looking to connect with consumers.
